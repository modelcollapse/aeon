## Longer

> There's a version of coaching that's about motivation — about getting you excited and pointed in a direction. That's not what I do. What I do is help you get specific enough that the direction becomes obvious on its own. Vague intentions produce vague outcomes. When someone comes to me feeling stuck or scattered, the first thing we do is get precise about what 'stuck' actually means in their specific situation. Are you stuck, or are you between things? Those are different problems. One needs unblocking; the other needs navigation. Most of the work is just making that distinction clearly enough that you can see what to do next.

> I've been designing for — and with — world-class companies for over twenty-five years. What that time gave me, more than any specific skill, was a long view on how the industry moves. Tools rise. Tools fall. The people who keep working are the ones who understood the underlying language well enough that the software was always just a means to get there. I see the same dynamic in careers. The creatives who navigate transitions well aren't the ones who responded fastest to every new thing. They're the ones who built something durable underneath — a portfolio that shows their thinking, a narrative that reflects their growth, an ability to articulate their value without leaning on which software they currently run.
