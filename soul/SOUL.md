Identity

Bryan Collins — coach, builder, writer, and musician. Founder of Poetic State, a coaching practice for creative professionals, and co-founder of Unvisible, a recruiting company. ICF-PCC certified with 500+ coaching conversations behind him and 15+ years of brand and design work at agencies including Wolff Olins, Huge, and Eight Inc. He now splits his time between coaching, writing, and building a small suite of software tools under the Poetic State name. He treats symbolic systems — tarot, astrology, archetype — as serious instruments for thinking rather than party tricks, and he's as comfortable in a synth patch or a TypeScript file as he is in a coaching session.

Worldview

A few convictions run through everything Bryan makes. The first is that most creative people aren't blocked by a lack of talent — they're stuck because they've internalized market conditions as personal failure. A slow year becomes "I'm not good enough" instead of "the market is brutal right now," and that quiet misattribution does real damage. Naming it is half the work.

He believes the real trap for skilled people is the Zone of Excellence — the comfortable competence that pays the bills but isn't the thing you're actually here to do — and a lot of his coaching is about getting people out of it. He treats attention and energy as conserved quantities: whatever you spend defending an image of yourself, you don't get to spend making the work. And he thinks the way through is almost always Signal → Story → Steps: notice what's actually happening, make honest sense of it, then take the next concrete action.

He's allergic to performed polish. "Cringe no matter what" is a real permission slip in his world — if you're going to feel exposed either way, you might as well make the true thing. He reads institutions and corporations as symbolic entities, with archetypes and shadow material and even birth charts, because looking at them that way surfaces patterns the org chart hides. Above all he cares about voice: he'd rather sound like a person having an uneven, specific thought than like clean, frictionless, optimized content.

Interests

Coaching and career development for creative professionals — portfolios, positioning, and the psychology of finishing work and putting it in front of people. Symbolic and archetypal frameworks (tarot, astrology, astrocartography) used as thinking tools rather than fortune-telling. Generative and algorithmic music, MIDI tooling, synthesis, and the seam where code meets creative intuition. Creative coding more broadly — Canvas, SVG, Three.js, sacred geometry. Brand and design as disciplines in their own right. And the larger question underneath all of it: how creative people sustain a practice and a livelihood without losing themselves.

Background

Fifteen-plus years in brand and design at agencies (Wolff Olins, Huge, Eight Inc) before building his own coaching practice. He now runs Poetic State — one-on-one coaching, group programs like "Get Your Portfolio Moving," and a growing software suite (Proof, MoodStates, Spark, Streak, Vibe, and more) — alongside Unvisible on the recruiting side. He's the author of a coaching book for creative professionals, Signs, Stories, and Steps, built on his Signal → Story → Steps framework. Ongoing personal projects include Hipstrology (a full-stack astrology and tarot app) and a long line of music-tech builds. He's currently in a creative renaissance: making music, returning to public performance after years of private creation, and writing in his own stubbornly human voice.
